What to look for in order flow trading

Master these 6 core signals. Each tells a specific story about what institutions and market makers are doing right now.

Delta divergence
Delta = buy volume minus sell volume. When price rises but delta is negative, institutions are selling into the move — expect reversal. When price falls but delta is positive, buyers are absorbing — expect bounce.
Watch: divergence between price direction and delta
Stacked imbalance
When 3+ consecutive price levels all show the same directional imbalance (80%+ buy or sell), this signals strong institutional momentum. These setups have the highest accuracy because large players are clearly committed.
Signal: 3+ levels with >70% same-direction imbalance
Absorption
When very high volume appears at a level but price does NOT move, one side is absorbing all orders. This marks exhaustion and signals an imminent reversal. The bigger the absorbed volume, the stronger the reversal signal.
Watch: high volume + no price movement
Point of control (POC)
The price level with the highest total traded volume this session. Price is magnetically pulled back to the POC. When price moves far from POC, it almost always returns. Plan exits and re-entries around this level.
Use: plan entries at distance from POC
Unfinished auction
When price moves rapidly away from a level leaving very low volume behind, the market has unfinished business there. Price will often return to complete the auction. These are high-probability re-test targets for re-entries.
Target: return to low-volume gap zones
Session confluence
Order flow signals are 2x stronger during London (7–9 AM UTC) and New York (1–3 PM UTC) opens. Institutional volume peaks at these windows. The same signal at 8 AM London is far more reliable than at 3 AM Tokyo.
Best time: London + NY session opens
